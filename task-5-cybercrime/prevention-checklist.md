# Stay Safe Online: The Essential Checklist for Indian College Students
## A Comprehensive Prevention Framework for Digital Security

As a student in a highly digitized academic landscape, your personal and financial data are constantly at risk. This checklist provides 8+ actionable strategies to fortify your digital presence and protect your academic career.

---

## 1. Financial & UPI Safety (Critical)

| Tip ID | Actionable Prevention Tip | Technical Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **FIN-01** | **The "No PIN for Receiving" Rule** | Never enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. Legitimate credits to your account happen automatically without your authentication. |
| **FIN-02** | **Set Daily UPI Transaction Limits** | Use your banking app to cap daily UPI transfers at a low amount (e.g., ₹5,000). This limits the "blast radius" if your device is compromised. |
| **FIN-03** | **Disable "Screen Sharing" for Payments** | Never keep apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer active while performing a transaction. Fraudsters use these to "shoulder surf" your PIN remotely. |

## 2. Academic & Identity Protection

### Tip 04: Secure Your University Portal
- [ ] **Action:** Use a unique, complex password for your college ERP/Student Portal that is different from your Gmail or social media.
- [ ] **Why:** If a peer or "hacker" gains access to your portal, they can manipulate your attendance, internal marks, or personal contact details.

### Tip 05: Public/Hostel Wi-Fi Hygiene
- [ ] **Action:** Use a reputable VPN (Virtual Private Network) when accessing bank accounts or sensitive emails on hostel or campus Wi-Fi.
- [ ] **Why:** Shared networks are vulnerable to "Man-in-the-Middle" (MitM) attacks where your data packets can be intercepted by others on the same router.

---

## 3. Social Media & Communication Safety

### Tip 06: The "Two-Factor" Mandate (2FA)
- [ ] **Action:** Enable App-based 2FA (like Google Authenticator) on WhatsApp, Instagram, and LinkedIn. 
- [ ] **Why:** Even if someone steals your password, they cannot bypass the time-sensitive code on your physical device.

### Tip 07: Verify "Urgent" Requests from Peers
- [ ] **Action:** If a friend messages you on WhatsApp/Telegram asking for an emergency loan or a "medical favor," call them on a direct phone line to verify.
- [ ] **Why:** Account hijacking is common; the person messaging you may not be your friend, but an attacker using their profile to scam their contact list.

### Tip 08: Beware of "Job/Internship" Phishing
- [ ] **Action:** Only apply for internships via official company websites or verified platforms like LinkedIn/Naukri. Ignore "Work from Home" offers sent via SMS or Telegram.
- [ ] **Why:** These are often "Task Scams" designed to steal your initial "registration fee" or your KYC documents for identity theft.

## 4. Device & Software Integrity

* **Update Regularly:** Keep your Android/iOS and Windows/macOS updated. These updates often contain critical security patches for "Zero-Day" vulnerabilities.
* **App Permissions:** Periodically check which apps have access to your Microphone, Camera, and Contacts. Revoke permissions for apps that don't need them.
* **Official Stores Only:** Never "Sideload" or download APKs from third-party websites. These files often contain hidden Trojans that log your keystrokes.

---

## 5. The "Red Flag" Mental Checklist

Before clicking any link, ask yourself:
1.  **Is it too good to be true?** (e.g., Free iPhones, "Get Rich Quick" schemes).
2.  **Is there a false sense of urgency?** (e.g., "Account will be blocked in 2 hours").
3.  **Is the sender's email/handle slightly "off"?** (e.g., @gmaiil.com or @company-hr-dept.net).

---

## 6. Official Reporting Channels (Save These)

If you have been cheated or notice suspicious activity, do not hesitate. In India, cybercrimes must be reported through official government channels to ensure legal action and potential fund reversal.

### **Immediate Actions:**
1.  **Call the National Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24/7). This is the fastest way to "freeze" stolen funds in the banking system.
2.  **File an Online Complaint:** Visit the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in).
3.  **Local Police:** Visit the nearest 'Cyber Cell' or Police Station to file a Physical FIR if the financial loss is significant.
